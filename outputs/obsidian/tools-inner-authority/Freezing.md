# Freezing

**Definition:** A sudden psychophysiological stress response triggered by perceived social judgment, manifesting as acute somatic inhibition, dry mouth, shakiness, and cognitive paralysis. It represents the physiological collapse of expressive capacity when an individual desperately attempts to hide their Shadow from an audience.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Freezing]] is the somatic manifestation of a psychological overload. It occurs when the nervous system cannot manage the conflicting desires to perform for an [[Outer Authority]] while simultaneously suppressing [[The Shadow]]. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Increased by the perceived power and pressure of [[Outer Authority]], fueled by chronic underlying [[Insecurity]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Triggers further social alienation and embarrassment, which violently increases [[Insecurity]] and perpetuates [[The Paralyzing Cycle of Insecurity]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Freezing acts as a severe negative constraint on **Capability** (both biological and cognitive inhibition). The user must possess the psychological bandwidth to override this somatic block. Recognizing the physiological symptoms of Freezing serves as the explicit environmental/internal cue (**Opportunity** / **Motivation**) prompting the individual to deploy the [[Inner Authority]] tool.
