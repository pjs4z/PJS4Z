# Psychological Dissipative Structure

**Definition:** A cognitive framework or transformational coping skill that functions analogously to biological/physical dissipative systems (like plants or engines) by extracting useful energy from psychological waste or entropy, recycling it into a stronger, more complex self-structure.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
Just as plants capture dissipating solar energy to create complex cellular structures, the [[Psychological Dissipative Structure]] captures the cognitive chaos of negative events. It forms the structural backbone of the [[Entropy Dissipation Mechanism]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Developed by an [[Autotelic Self]] through the maturation of [[Unselfconscious Self-Assurance]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Acts as the primary prerequisite for [[Transformational Coping]], actively neutralizing [[Psychic Entropy]] and preventing [[Regressive Coping]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Acts primarily as a **Capability** driver. Without this mental structure, individuals lack the cognitive framing required to process failure as anything other than a threat.
- **Leverage Point:** *Ego-Decoupling Exercises* can help individuals reframe success not as dominating the environment, but as harmoniously participating within it. This builds the dissipative capacity to absorb "shocks" and translate them into usable [[Psychic Energy]].
