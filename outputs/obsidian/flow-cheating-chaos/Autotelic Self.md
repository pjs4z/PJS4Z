# Autotelic Self

**Definition:** A complex personality structure characterized by self-contained, internally generated goals rather than those dictated by biological needs or social conventions. It enables an individual to maintain inner harmony by readily translating potential threats and adversity into enjoyable challenges.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Autotelic Self]] is the central hub for directing [[Psychic Energy]] efficiently. Instead of succumbing to [[Psychic Entropy]] when faced with adversity, this personality structure employs a [[Psychological Dissipative Structure]] to process negative feedback.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Repeated successful iterations of the [[Autotelic Growth Cycle]], accumulation of [[Unselfconscious Self-Assurance]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Constantly catalyzes [[Flow]] (optimal experience) by initiating [[Transformational Coping]] in the face of environmental stressors.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In behavioral terms, the [[Autotelic Self]] serves as a massive **Reflective Motivation** driver. When default or biologically programmed goals are thwarted, this self-structure consciously formulates new, self-concordant micro-goals. 
- **Leverage Point:** Interventions should focus on *Micro-Goal Scaffolding*, prompting individuals to immediately set achievable, internally-driven goals the moment a primary goal fails, thus jumpstarting their intrinsic motivation.
