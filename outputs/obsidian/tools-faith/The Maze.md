# The Maze

**Definition:** A rigid, cyclical cognitive state characterized by endless loops of obsession, imaginary arguments, and defensive resentment that traps and consumes an individual's emotional bandwidth.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[The Maze]] is a pathological state of cognitive capture that is significantly increased by adherence to a [[Mechanistic Worldview]]. This state feeds skepticism and isolation, creating a systemic barrier to psychological relief. It is the primary target of [[Active Love]], which acts to decrease its hold. [[The Maze]] forms the core of the [[Rationalist Resentment Trap]], a reinforcing loop where resentment fosters defensiveness, isolating the individual from the tools needed to escape it. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Mechanistic Worldview]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Active Love]] (decreases when deployed against it), reinforces [[Mechanistic Worldview]] (via the Rationalist Resentment Trap)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In behavioral engineering, [[The Maze]] typically acts as a major capability inhibitor, stripping the user of cognitive bandwidth and emotional flexibility. 
- **Leverage Point:** The most effective intervention is a cognitive reframe: transforming the onset of [[The Maze]] from an inescapable psychological trap into an explicit behavioral cue. By teaching individuals to identify their own obsessive, resentful loops, the adversity itself becomes the explicit trigger (Opportunity) to practice [[Active Love]].
