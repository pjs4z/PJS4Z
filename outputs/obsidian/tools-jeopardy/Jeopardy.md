# Jeopardy

**Definition:** A psychological visualization process that invokes one's deathbed self to create an urgent awareness of limited time. This urgency triggers the self-generated willpower needed to act in the present moment and avoid squandering one's potential.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Jeopardy]] operates as a critical intervention mechanism, functioning as a deliberate Occurrent to break negative behavioral loops. It serves as the primary engine within the [[The Creator's Willpower Engine]] feedback loop.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Demoralization]] (which acts as the painful catalyst or "darkness" requiring the tool) and [[The Creator]] (the identity archetype that consistently applies it).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Generates [[Willpower]] and actively inhibits the passivity associated with [[The Consumer]]. 

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In COM-B terms, [[Jeopardy]] acts as a potent driver of both Automatic and Reflective Motivation. Automatically, it triggers a visceral, primal fear of a squandered life. Reflectively, it dismantles the illusion of [[Exoneration]]. 
- **Leverage Point:** Establish "If-Then" implementation intentions. Use the internal symptoms of [[Demoralization]] (e.g., the urge to quit) or the illusion of [[Exoneration]] (e.g., the feeling of "I've made it") as automatic internal triggers to immediately deploy the [[Jeopardy]] visualization.
- **Leverage Point:** Use the tool preemptively during high-risk scenarios (e.g., facing distraction from [[Consumerism]]) to pre-load the nervous system with active urgency.
