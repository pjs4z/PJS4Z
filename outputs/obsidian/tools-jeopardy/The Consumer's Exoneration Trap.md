# The Consumer's Exoneration Trap

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop where consumerist culture feeds the fantasy of an effort-free life, inevitably leading to disillusionment, demoralization, and deeper susceptibility to consumerist quick-fixes.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop explains the pervasive failure of self-help and continuous improvement systems when subjected to societal defaults. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Ubiquitous [[Consumerism]] actively increases [[The Consumer]] archetype.
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[The Consumer]] actively seeks [[Exoneration]]. Because an effort-free life is impossible, this illusion shatters, spiking [[Demoralization]]. High demoralization decreases [[Willpower]], leaving the individual paralyzed and even more entrenched as a consumer, hungry for the next false promise.

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop severely degrades the Physical and Social Opportunity for genuine growth. The environment is engineered to interrupt self-reflection.
- **Leverage Point:** Engineer the physical and digital environment to introduce high friction against [[Consumerism]] (e.g., app blockers, delayed gratification rules). 
- **Leverage Point:** Substitute the ubiquitous consumerist triggers with "memento mori" prompts embedded in daily routines. This constantly disrupts the trap by invoking [[Jeopardy]], shifting the individual toward [[The Creator]] archetype.
