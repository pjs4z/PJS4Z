# Psychic Entropy

**Definition:** The default, chaotic state of human consciousness that emerges when the mind is devoid of external stimuli or internal discipline, causing attention to drift randomly toward painful, disturbing, or problematic thoughts.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Psychic Entropy]] represents the mind's baseline condition when not actively managed. It serves as the primary catalyst for the [[Consciousness Ordering Homeostasis]] loop, wherein cognitive discomfort forces the system to seek order.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Lack of structured external stimuli, unallocated [[Psychic Energy]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Consciousness Ordering Homeostasis]] (triggers compensatory structuring)
- **Mitigated by:** [[Mental Flow]], [[Memory]], [[Daydreaming]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the COM-B model, [[Psychic Entropy]] functions as a powerful, albeit negative, **Motivation** driver; individuals consciously or unconsciously seek to escape the emotional discomfort of an undisciplined mind. Environmentally (**Opportunity**), modern life offers low-effort attention sinks (like television) that artificially mask this entropy. A powerful behavioral leverage point is to intentionally design "high-entropy, low-distraction" environments by removing passive media. This temporarily exposes the individual to [[Psychic Entropy]], forcing the active, compensatory use of an internalized [[Symbolic System]] to generate true [[Mental Flow]].
