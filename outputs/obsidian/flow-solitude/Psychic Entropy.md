# Psychic Entropy

**Definition:** A state of internal mental chaos and disorganization that naturally arises when external goals are lacking, allowing negative thoughts and worries to dominate consciousness.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Psychic Entropy]] is the default state of the unstructured mind when left in isolation. Biological [[Gregariousness]] makes solitude uncomfortable, catalyzing [[Ontological Anxiety]], which in turn increases the baseline of [[Psychic Entropy]]. To counteract this internal chaos, an individual must invest [[Psychic Energy]] into complex goals to achieve a [[Flow Experience]], which acts as a powerful inhibitor of entropy. If the individual fails to structure their mind, they fall into [[The Social Entropy and Regression Loop]], relying on passive distractions that cause further loss of consciousness control.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Ontological Anxiety]], [[Gregariousness]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Passive distractions (e.g., TV, drug use) *[when unmitigated]*, inhibited by [[Flow Experience]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of behavioral design, [[Psychic Entropy]] acts as a potent **Motivation** (specifically, an aversive "push" to avoid mental chaos). Because the mind naturally seeks to escape this discomfort, individuals without the **Capability** (such as an [[Autotelic Personality]] or strong [[Instrumental Skills]] and [[Expressive Skills]]) will seize the lowest-effort **Opportunity** to numb the chaos (e.g., turning on a screen). To properly engineer behavior away from regression, leverage points like *Environmental Scaffolding for Solitude* must be applied—removing low-effort distractions so the individual is gently forced to invest [[Psychic Energy]] into growth-oriented tasks.
