# The Maze

**Definition:** A debilitating, repetitive cognitive and emotional process triggered by a perceived interpersonal injury. In this state, an individual becomes obsessively trapped in rumination, hurt, and retaliatory fantasies, effectively halting their forward progression in life.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[The Maze]] acts as a powerful psychological sinkhole, fundamentally disrupting a person's ability to engage with the present moment. It operates through a reinforcing feedback loop known as **The Maze Entrapment**: the presence of a rigid [[Fairness Expectation]] catalyzes entry into this state when an interpersonal injustice is perceived. Once inside, rumination and demands for justice heavily reinforce the childish [[Fairness Expectation]], driving the individual deeper into cognitive entrapment.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Fairness Expectation]] (catalyzes the entry into this state).
- **Downstream (Drives):** Inhibits [[Active Love]] (the reactive and self-righteous nature of the state blocks the very tool needed to escape it). Decreased and starved by the generation of [[Outflow]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of behavior change, [[The Maze]] acts as a profound barrier to both psychological flexibility and the target behavior of initiating [[Active Love]].
- **Motivation Barrier:** It creates intense *Automatic Motivation* (self-righteous rage, retaliatory urges) that overrides reflective, goal-directed behavior.
- **Leverage Point:** Escaping this state requires reframing the intervention not as a moral obligation ("doing the right thing" for the offender), but as an act of pure self-interest to preserve one's own mental bandwidth and future potential.
