# The Self

**Definition:** A dynamic mental representation residing within consciousness that encompasses an individual's memories, actions, desires, and their entire constructed hierarchy of goals. It directs attention while simultaneously being continuously shaped by how attention is invested.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[The Self]] is governed by circular causality: it creates the boundaries of subjective experience while being built by those very experiences. Through the *Flow-Complexity Spiral*, it evolves into a state of [[Self-Complexity]] by balancing [[Differentiation]] and [[Integration]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Increased and fortified by [[Attention]] (when directed successfully) and [[Flow]] (which adds structural integrity and complexity). Decreased and disorganized by [[Psychic Entropy]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes [[Attention]] (acting as the captain that directs psychic energy toward specific goals).

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability:** Represents the structural resilience and cognitive bandwidth required to evaluate disruptive information without becoming overwhelmed.
- **Leverage Point (Goal Priming):** To enable the conscious override of automatic [[Biological Programming]], behavioral systems should continuously prime the user's highest-order goals. Strengthening the salience of these goals gives [[The Self]] the psychological leverage needed to maintain focus under pressure.
