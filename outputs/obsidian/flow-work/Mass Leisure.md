# Mass Leisure

**Definition:** Commercially designed, passive forms of entertainment that act as parasites of the mind, absorbing psychic energy and mimicking reality without providing substantive psychological strength or growth in return.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Mass Leisure]] serves as the primary behavioral sink in [[The Parasitic Leisure Drain]] feedback loop. During unstructured free time, a lack of immediate goals leads individuals toward these commercially designed occurrences. Engaging in [[Mass Leisure]] acts as a parasite that absorbs and depletes the individual's Stock of [[Psychic Energy]], directly increasing the likelihood of experiencing [[Apathy]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Unstructured free time, lack of clear goals
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Apathy]], Decreases [[Psychic Energy]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Mass Leisure]] acts as a pervasive **Opportunity** barrier, outcompeting more complex, growth-oriented hobbies because it requires zero activation energy to consume.
- **Leverage Point (Choice Architecture):** Introduce high environmental friction to [[Mass Leisure]] access (e.g., placing televisions in inconvenient locations, utilizing app-blockers) to prevent the effortless drain of [[Psychic Energy]].
- **Leverage Point (Feedback Implementation):** Counteract [[Apathy]] in leisure by introducing tracking systems for personal hobbies, converting unstructured time into intrinsically rewarding pursuits that compete with [[Mass Leisure]].
