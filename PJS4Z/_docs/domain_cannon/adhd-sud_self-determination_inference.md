# Canonical frameworks for an ADHD-SUD self-determination inference engine

A curated set of **12 high-leverage theoretical frameworks** — spanning motivational science, behavior change methodology, neuroscience, cognitive/social ontology, and knowledge representation — can ground a semi-formal Personal OS that models self-determination vectors in adults with ADHD-linked substance use disorder. The vital insight unifying this set is that three independently well-validated theoretical traditions converge on the same explanatory junction: Self-Determination Theory provides the motivational architecture, incentive salience theory provides the neurobiological substrate, and social identity models of recovery provide the relational/contextual dynamics. No existing system integrates these three at the ontological level — making this a genuine gap. What follows is a Pareto-minimal reference list organized from meta-model backbone through domain-specific layers, with explicit inter-framework mappings.

---

## The ontological backbone: UFO, design patterns, and temporal algebra

The single highest-leverage choice for the upper ontology is **UFO (Unified Foundational Ontology)**, developed by Giancarlo Guizzardi and colleagues (Guizzardi et al., 2022, *Applied Ontology* 17(1), 167–210; Guizzardi, 2005, PhD thesis, University of Twente). UFO's three strata map directly onto the system's requirements. **UFO-A** (structural) provides objects, qualities, modes, and critically, *dispositions* — realisable entities that bridge stable traits and dynamic events. ADHD impulsivity, substance cravings, and motivational orientations are all naturally modeled as dispositions that get realized under specific triggering conditions. **UFO-B** (events/processes) provides event mereology, temporal ordering, participation, and causation, including the disposition-event realization link. **UFO-C** (social/intentional) provides beliefs, desires, intentions, goals, commitments, social roles, and social relators — precisely the constructs Self-Determination Theory formalizes at the psychological level. A lightweight implementation, **gUFO** (Almeida et al., 2019), provides an OWL 2 DL module ready for semi-formal reuse without requiring full formal reasoning.

UFO's advantage over alternatives is decisive for this use case. BFO (Basic Formal Ontology; Arp, Smith & Spear, 2015, MIT Press; ISO/IEC 21838-2:2021) is the most widely adopted upper ontology — over 650 projects use it, and the BCIO and Mental Functioning Ontology are BFO-conformant — but BFO's realist stance and sparse treatment of intentional/social entities make it a weaker fit for modeling motivational states. DOLCE (Borgo et al., 2022, *Applied Ontology* 17(1), 45–69) offers the valuable **Descriptions & Situations (D&S) pattern** for modeling context-dependent situations (recovery scenarios, relapse triggers, therapeutic encounters), and DOLCE-Ultralite (DUL) provides a lightweight OWL implementation. The pragmatic recommendation: **use UFO/gUFO as the structural backbone, import the D&S pattern from DOLCE for situation modeling, and align where needed with BFO categories to maintain interoperability** with existing biomedical ontologies.

Two additional meta-level frameworks complete the backbone. **Ontology Design Patterns** (Gangemi & Presutti, 2009, in *Handbook on Ontologies*, Springer; Blomqvist, Hammar & Presutti, 2016, IOS Press) provide modular, reusable ontology fragments — Participation, Sequence, TimeIndexedSituation, RecurrentSituation — that enable incremental construction without monolithic design. The eXtreme Design (XD) methodology supports agile, middle-out development starting from core domain concepts. **Allen's temporal interval algebra** (Allen, 1983, *CACM* 26(11), 832–843) provides 13 binary temporal relations (before, during, overlaps, meets, etc.) that are intuitive enough for LLM consumption in natural language: "craving_episode DURING stress_period" or "treatment_milestone AFTER identity_shift." OWL-Time (W3C) provides Semantic Web implementation if needed.

## Self-Determination Theory as the primary motivational layer

SDT is the single most important theoretical commitment in the system. The definitive reference is **Ryan & Deci (2017), *Self-Determination Theory: Basic Psychological Needs in Motivation, Development, and Wellness*, Guilford Press** — a 756-page handbook covering all six mini-theories with full empirical review. Three of the six mini-theories carry the heaviest modeling weight.

**Organismic Integration Theory (OIT)** provides the primary state dimension: the internalization continuum from amotivation through external regulation, introjected regulation, identified regulation, and integrated regulation to intrinsic motivation (Ryan & Connell, 1989, *JPSP* 57, 749–761). For ADHD-SUD recovery, this continuum is where the action is. Recovery motivation shifts from external (court-ordered, family pressure) through introjected (shame/guilt-driven) to identified (personally valued) to integrated (recovery as authentic self-expression). The Treatment Self-Regulation Questionnaire (TSRQ) operationalizes this continuum. OIT is the dimension that answers "where is this person motivationally and why."

**Basic Psychological Needs Theory (BPNT)** provides the three continuous need-satisfaction/frustration dimensions — **autonomy, competence, relatedness** — that form a core state vector predicting both vulnerability and resilience (Vansteenkiste, Ryan & Soenens, 2020, *Motivation and Emotion* 44, 1–31). Need frustration is a primary predictor of substance use as compensatory coping. For ADHD specifically: chronic **competence frustration** from repeated failures plus **autonomy frustration** from controlling environments creates heightened SUD vulnerability.

**Causality Orientations Theory (COT)** provides a trait-level baseline modifier — three personality orientations (autonomy, control, impersonal) that modulate how environmental inputs translate into behavior (Deci & Ryan, 1985, *J. Research in Personality* 19, 109–134). A 2024 scoping review (Herchenroeder et al., *Substance Use & Misuse* 59(10)) found COT is the most-studied SDT component in non-treatment substance use research. Impersonal orientation (amotivation/helplessness) predicts worst substance use outcomes.

Three complementary motivational frameworks extend SDT's coverage without adding redundant complexity. **Sheldon's Self-Concordance Model** (Sheldon & Elliot, 1999, *JPSP* 76(3), 482–497) adds a goal-level state variable — whether recovery goals are authentically chosen or externally imposed. Low self-concordance predicts dropout. Recent work (Sheldon & Holberg, 2022, *Current Opinion in Psychology*) connects self-concordance to system 1/system 2 alignment, directly relevant to ADHD's impaired reflective integration. **Markus & Nurius's Possible Selves** (1986, *American Psychologist* 41(9), 954–969) adds identity-level state variables: the "recovered self" as hoped-for possible self and the "addicted self" as feared possible self create approach-avoidance dynamics. Balanced possible selves (both hoped-for and feared in the same domain) are most motivationally effective. **Carver & Scheier's self-regulation theory** (1998, *On the Self-Regulation of Behavior*, Cambridge) provides the cybernetic control-system architecture — the discrepancy between current state and reference value generates motivational energy. For ADHD, this loop is specifically impaired: poor self-monitoring delays discrepancy detection, and poor self-regulation impairs corrective action even when discrepancy is detected.

A critical bridge paper links SDT directly to ADHD: **Champ, Adamou & Tolchard (2022), *Psychological Review* 130(4)** proposes a comprehensive SDT-based framework for ADHD, arguing that ADHD symptomatology arises from chronic need frustration rather than being purely neurocognitive deficits. Morsink et al. (2022, *J. Attention Disorders* 26(8), 1139–1158) provides the complementary mapping between existing ADHD neurocognitive models and SDT constructs. Together, these papers provide the theoretical translation layer between neurobiological state and motivational state.

## Behavior change architecture: COM-B, PRIME, and psychological flexibility

The **COM-B model** (Michie, van Stralen & West, 2011, *Implementation Science* 6, 42) provides the minimal sufficient state space for behavioral analysis: six sub-components across three conditions — Physical and Psychological **Capability**, Physical and Social **Opportunity**, Reflective and Automatic **Motivation**. ADHD-specific deficits map cleanly: executive function impairment maps to Psychological Capability, impulsivity to Automatic Motivation, environmental structure to Physical Opportunity. COM-B's dynamic feedback loops (behavior changes capability/opportunity/motivation over time) are essential for modeling recovery trajectories.

COM-B's Motivation component is elaborated by **Robert West's PRIME theory** (West, 2006, *Theory of Addiction*, Blackwell; West & Brown, 2013, 2nd ed., Wiley), which specifies a five-level motivational hierarchy: **P**lans → **R**esponses → **I**mpulses/Inhibitions → **M**otives → **E**valuations. PRIME adds two concepts missing from SDT: (1) identity shifts as motivational phase transitions (the "smoker" → "non-smoker" shift restructures the entire motivational economy), and (2) chaos-theory-like dynamics where small perturbations can trigger large behavioral changes. PRIME explicitly integrates with COM-B and provides the motivational microstructure the inference engine needs for modeling moment-to-moment state transitions.

The **UCL Behaviour Change Intervention Ontology (BCIO)** (Michie et al., 2021, *Wellcome Open Research* 5:126) serves not as a framework to import wholesale but as a **structural template**. It is the only formally structured ontology in the behavior change field encoded in OWL with BFO alignment. Its Mechanism of Action Ontology (Schenk et al., 2024, *Wellcome Open Research* 8:337) — 284 entities across 7 hierarchical levels — directly models psychological processes (motivation, self-regulation, beliefs, emotions). The companion Addiction Ontology (Hastings, Cox, West & Notley, 2020, Qeios) demonstrates how BFO categories disambiguate addiction terminology like "craving," "motivation," and "social norm" — exactly the disambiguation the Personal OS needs.

**Hayes's psychological flexibility model** (Hayes, Strosahl & Wilson, 2012, *Acceptance and Commitment Therapy*, 2nd ed., Guilford) adds six process dimensions via the hexaflex: acceptance, cognitive defusion, present-moment contact, self-as-context, values, and committed action. Each has a corresponding inflexibility pole. Experiential avoidance (the inflexible pole of acceptance) is a **core mechanism maintaining SUD** — substance use functions as avoidance of painful internal experience. The bridge to SDT is direct: values clarification in ACT is functionally equivalent to SDT's identified/integrated regulation; committed action parallels autonomous motivation. The Extended Evolutionary Meta-Model (Hayes, Hofmann & Ciarrochi, 2020, *Clinical Psychology Review* 82, 101908) organizes processes across psychological dimensions and levels of analysis, providing a meta-model compatible with the layered architecture.

**Recovery Capital** (Granfield & Cloud, 1999/2001; Cloud & Granfield, 2008, *Substance Use & Misuse* 43, 1971–1986; Best & Hennessy, 2022, *Addiction* 117(4), 1139–1145) provides the resource-state model: personal, social, and community capital (plus negative recovery capital as barrier states). The capital metaphor is inherently quantitative — accumulation, spending, investment — and maps to COM-B cleanly: personal capital maps to Capability + Motivation, social capital to Social Opportunity, community capital to Physical Opportunity. SAMHSA's four recovery dimensions (Health, Home, Purpose, Community) provide coarse-grained life-domain categories at the outermost layer.

## Neuroscience as the explanatory substrate

Three frameworks provide neurobiological grounding without requiring full biomedical ontology complexity.

**Robinson & Berridge's Incentive Salience Theory** (1993, *Brain Research Reviews* 18(3), 247–291; 2016, *American Psychologist* 71(8), 670–679; 2025, *Annual Review of Psychology* 76, 29–58) provides the cleanest neuroscience abstraction: the **"wanting" / "liking" / "learning" trichotomy**. "Wanting" (incentive salience, mediated by mesolimbic dopamine) is dissociable from "liking" (hedonic impact, opioid-mediated) and from "learning" (associative). Incentive sensitization from repeated drug exposure amplifies wanting without increasing liking. This maps directly to the ADHD-SUD junction: ADHD involves baseline hypodopaminergic tone (reduced wanting for non-drug rewards), while repeated substance use sensitizes the wanting system for drug cues specifically. Three state variables capture the essential dynamics: **incentive_salience_level, hedonic_capacity, sensitization_degree**.

**Koob & Le Moal's allostatic model** (2001, *Neuropsychopharmacology* 24, 97–129; 2008, *Annual Review of Psychology* 59, 29–53) explains why the motivational landscape shifts over time. The reward set point drifts downward through opponent processes and anti-reward system activation (CRF, noradrenaline, dynorphin). The three-stage addiction cycle — binge/intoxication (basal ganglia), withdrawal/negative affect (extended amygdala), preoccupation/anticipation (PFC) — provides a natural state machine. Key state variables: **reward_set_point, allostatic_load, addiction_stage**. The allostatic model connects to SDT through need frustration: as hedonic tone drops, basic psychological needs become harder to satisfy through non-drug means, driving compensatory substance use.

**Barkley's executive function model** (1997, *Psychological Bulletin* 121(1), 65–94; 2012, *Executive Functions*, Guilford) provides the capacity constraints on the SDT system. Behavioral inhibition deficit cascades into impaired working memory, internalized speech, affect/motivation regulation, and reconstitution. This directly explains **why OIT internalization is impaired in ADHD**: working memory limitations reduce the ability to hold long-term goals online, impaired motivational self-regulation reduces capacity for sustained effort toward identified goals, and impaired speech internalization reduces the reflective processing needed for value integration. Barkley's model also connects to **delay discounting** (Jackson & MacKillop, 2016, *Biological Psychiatry: CNNI* 1(4), 316–325), which is a robust shared transdiagnostic intermediate phenotype for ADHD-SUD comorbidity with effect sizes of d ≈ 0.4–0.6.

The **self-medication hypothesis** (Khantzian, 1985, *Am. J. Psychiatry* 142(11), 1259–1264; 1997, *Harvard Review of Psychiatry* 4(5), 231–244) reframes the ADHD-SUD link: substance choice reflects interaction between psychopharmacology and dominant painful feelings. ADHD individuals use stimulants to relieve dysphoria, anhedonia, and executive dysfunction — not for euphoria but for normal functioning. This reframing is critical for the intentional stance: substance use in this population is often rational self-medication, not mere hedonism.

## Cognitive and social layers for modeling agents in recovery

**Dennett's Intentional Stance** (1987, *The Intentional Stance*, MIT Press) provides the philosophical license for the entire system. The intentional stance treats entities as rational agents with beliefs, desires, and intentions without requiring neural-level fidelity — it is explicitly a pragmatic predictive strategy. This is the ontological commitment that permits the Personal OS to model motivational states using folk-psychological categories while remaining scientifically grounded. The intentional stance validates modeling at the "person level" rather than the "brain level."

The **BDI (Belief-Desire-Intention) model** (Bratman, 1987, *Intention, Plans, and Practical Reason*, Harvard; Rao & Georgeff, 1995, Proceedings of ICMAS) operationalizes the intentional stance computationally. Beliefs map to the agent's model of self and world (including perceived competence and perceived autonomy — SDT constructs). Desires map to basic needs satisfaction/frustration. Intentions map to committed plans with temporal persistence — and the quality of that commitment (autonomous vs. controlled) reflects the OIT internalization level. A landmark 2025 paper — **Zuppiroli et al., "The Belief-Desire-Intention Ontology for Modelling Mental Reality and Agency" (arXiv:2511.17162)** — provides an OWL ontology aligned with DOLCE-Ultralite and validates it via Logic Augmented Generation (LAG) with GPT-4o. The study showed that serializing ontological constraints into LLM prompts improved both inference coherence and contradiction detection. This is the closest existing proof of concept for the Personal OS approach.

The **Social Identity Model of Recovery (SIMOR)** (Best et al., 2016, *Addiction Research & Theory* 24(2), 111–123) and the related **Social Identity Model of Cessation Maintenance (SIMCM)** (Frings & Albery, 2015, *Addictive Behaviors* 44, 35–44) model recovery as social identity transition: from "addict identity" to "recovery identity." This connects directly to PRIME theory's identity mechanism and to Possible Selves theory — the transition from feared self to hoped-for self occurs through acquiring new group memberships that provide identity resources. Recovery communities (12-step groups, peer support networks) function as **social identity laboratories** where new identities are constructed, rehearsed, and reinforced. State variables: recovery_identity_strength, addict_identity_salience, group_membership_salience. Searle's social ontology (1995, *The Construction of Social Reality*, Free Press) provides the formal apparatus: the "X counts as Y in context C" constitutive rule explains how "sobriety" counts as valued status through collective acceptance, and how "sponsor" is a status function creating deontic powers.

The **Mental Functioning Ontology (MFO)** (Hastings et al., 2012, Proceedings of ICBO; available at github.com/jannahastings/mental-functioning-ontology) and its Emotion Ontology module provide a BFO-conformant vocabulary for cognitive and affective states. MFO models mental functioning as dispositions realized in processes — attention deficit becomes a disrupted cognitive process, impulsivity becomes a disposition with specific realization conditions. MFO's modular design allows importing only relevant fragments without carrying full biomedical ontology weight.

## Making the ontology LLM-friendly through Logic Augmented Generation

The critical question for the Personal OS is not just what to represent but how to make ontological knowledge consumable by an LLM reasoning engine. The BDI Ontology LAG validation (Zuppiroli et al., 2025) demonstrates the core pattern: **serialize ontological constraints as natural-language prompt templates**, then use the LLM's common-sense reasoning to draw inferences consistent with the ontological structure. This T2B2T (Triples-to-Beliefs-to-Triples) paradigm creates a bidirectional flow between structured knowledge and LLM reasoning.

Frame-based representations (descending from Minsky's frames and Schank's scripts) are experiencing a renaissance in LLM contexts because their slot-filler-default structure maps naturally to prompt templates. The Framester project (Gangemi et al., 2016, EKAW 2016) connects FrameNet frames to DOLCE-Zero, enabling frame-based reasoning over linked data. For the Personal OS, each motivational state can be represented as a frame with typed slots: "Recovery_Motivation_State [frame] has regulation_type [OIT position], need_satisfaction [BPNT vector], triggering_situation [D&S situation], executive_capacity [Barkley EF level], identity_salience [SIMOR identity]." An LLM can reason over such frames using common-sense inference: "Given that autonomy_satisfaction is LOW and regulation_type is INTROJECTED, predict that recovery_effort will be fragile and contingent on external monitoring."

**SKOS** (Miles & Bechhofer, 2009, W3C Recommendation) provides the surface vocabulary layer — organizing concepts via broader/narrower/related relations with human-readable labels. SKOS concepts are RDF individuals (not OWL classes), making them easy to annotate and extend. The practical architecture layers SKOS concept labels over UFO-structured typed relations, serialized into natural language templates for LLM consumption.

Knowledge Graph + LLM integration approaches — particularly **GraphRAG** (Edge et al., 2024, Microsoft Research) and ontology-enhanced reasoning (Pan et al., 2024, *IEEE TKDE*) — provide established patterns for LLM traversal of structured knowledge. The neuro-symbolic integration literature (Herron, Jiménez-Ruiz & Weyde, 2025, *ACM Computing Surveys*) confirms that the symbolic-to-LLM pipeline (injecting ontological knowledge into prompts) and the LLM-to-symbolic pipeline (extracting structured knowledge from outputs) create the bidirectional flow the Personal OS requires.

## How the 12 frameworks connect into a coherent architecture

The inter-framework relationships form a layered system with clear causal pathways:

| Layer | Frameworks | State Variables | Connects To |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Life domains** | SAMHSA 4 dimensions | Health, Home, Purpose, Community | Outermost context |
| **Behavioral system** | COM-B + PRIME | 6 COM-B sub-components; PRIME 5-level hierarchy | Behavioral diagnosis |
| **Motivational core** | SDT (OIT + BPNT + COT) | Regulation type, need satisfaction/frustration vector, causality orientation | Primary inference target |
| **Process dynamics** | ACT hexaflex + Self-Concordance | 6 flexibility dimensions; goal-self alignment | Explains motivational quality |
| **Identity** | SIMOR + Possible Selves | Recovery/addict identity salience; hoped-for/feared selves | Social context of motivation |
| **Neurobiological** | IST + Allostatic model + Barkley EF | Wanting/liking, reward set point, EF capacity | Capacity constraints on motivation |
| **Agentive** | BDI + Intentional Stance | Beliefs, desires, intentions | Inference architecture |
| **Structural** | UFO/gUFO + D&S + Allen temporal + MFO | Categories, situations, temporal relations, mental processes | Ontological backbone |

The five most critical causal pathways the inference engine must represent are: (1) **Barkley EF deficits constrain OIT internalization rate** — low working memory and self-regulation capacity limit how fast someone can move along the autonomy continuum; (2) **BPNT need frustration drives compensatory substance use** — chronic competence and autonomy frustration creates the motivational vacuum substances temporarily fill; (3) **IST sensitization amplifies wanting for drugs while allostatic load reduces hedonic capacity for non-drug rewards** — creating a progressively narrowing motivational landscape; (4) **SIMOR identity transition mediates sustained recovery** — the shift from addict identity to recovery identity restructures the entire motivational economy via PRIME's identity mechanism; (5) **Self-concordance predicts recovery durability** — externally imposed recovery goals (external/introjected OIT regulation) predict dropout, while self-concordant goals (identified/integrated regulation) predict sustained effort.

## Conclusion: a novel junction with no existing integration

This Pareto set is deliberately minimal. Twelve frameworks span six layers, connected by five core causal pathways, yielding a state space that is rich enough to model the complexity of ADHD-SUD recovery motivation but constrained enough for LLM-based common-sense inference. The 2024 Herchenroeder et al. scoping review found only 38 studies applying SDT to substance use — and none specifically addressing ADHD-SUD comorbidity through an SDT lens. Lintunen et al. (2025, *Motivation and Emotion*) explicitly notes that computational modeling has seen "little use" in SDT. The BDI Ontology LAG validation (2025) proves the concept of ontology-augmented LLM reasoning works but has not been applied to motivational state modeling. This Personal OS would operate at a junction — computational SDT, addiction recovery modeling, ADHD neurodiversity frameworks, and LLM-based inference — where essentially no existing work exists. The ontological backbone (UFO + D&S + Allen temporal) provides the structural vocabulary; the motivational layer (SDT + Self-Concordance + Possible Selves) provides the primary inference dimensions; the behavioral layer (COM-B/PRIME + ACT) provides the process dynamics; the neuroscience layer (IST + allostatic model + Barkley) provides capacity constraints; the social layer (SIMOR + Searle) provides relational context; and the LLM interface layer (LAG + frames + SKOS) makes it all machine-reasonable. The result is not an ontology in the strict formal sense but something potentially more useful for this purpose: a typed concept map with enough structure to constrain LLM reasoning and enough flexibility to accommodate the messy reality of human recovery.
