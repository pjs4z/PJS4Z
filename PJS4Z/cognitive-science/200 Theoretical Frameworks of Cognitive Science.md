# 200 Theoretical Frameworks of Cognitive Science

The semantic space of cognitive science is governed by competing and complementary theoretical frameworks (paradigms) that dictate *how* the mind is modeled. These paradigms serve as the structural foundation for the [[300 Frame Semantics Meta-Model of Cognition]].

### 1. [[Cognitivism (Computational Theory of Mind, GOFAI)]]
- **Core Metaphor:** The mind is a digital computer.
- **Premise:** Cognition is the rule-based, algorithmic manipulation of abstract, amodal symbols (representations).
- **Key Concepts:** Turing machines, physical symbol systems, modularity, Language of Thought (Fodor).

### 2. [[Connectionism (Parallel Distributed Processing)]]
- **Core Metaphor:** The mind is a dynamic neural network.
- **Premise:** Cognition emerges from the distributed, parallel activity of interconnected units. Rejects explicit symbols in favor of statistical pattern matching and connection weights.
- **Key Concepts:** Artificial Neural Networks, deep learning, Hebbian learning, backpropagation.

### 3. [[4E Cognition]]
- **Core Metaphor:** The mind is a coupled organism-environment system.
- **Premise:** Cognition is not just "in the head." It is **E**mbodied (relies on physical morphology), **E**mbedded (situated in environments), **E**xtended (offloaded to tools), and **E**nactive (generated through sense-making action).
- **Key Concepts:** Affordances, morphological computation, sensorimotor contingencies.

### 4. [[Predictive Processing (Active Inference)]]
- **Core Metaphor:** The mind is a Bayesian inference engine.
- **Premise:** The brain fundamentally attempts to minimize "prediction error" (surprise) by continuously updating its internal top-down models of the world or acting on the world to fulfill its predictions.
- **Key Concepts:** Free energy principle (Friston), Bayesian updating, Markov blankets.
