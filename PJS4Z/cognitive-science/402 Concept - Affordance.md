# 402 Concept - Affordance

> **Definition:** An affordance is an action possibility presented by the environment to an organism, strictly determined by the organism's specific physical capabilities. 

For example, a chair "affords" sitting to a human, but it affords hiding to a mouse. A doorknob affords twisting to a human hand.

## Frame Semantic Context
In our [[300 Frame Semantics Meta-Model of Cognition]], **Affordance** acts as a core Frame Element within the **[[303 Frame - Embodied Action]]**.

- It is perceived directly through continuous **`[COUPLING]`**.

## Theoretical Significance
Coined by James J. Gibson in ecological psychology, the concept of affordances breaks away from the Cognitivist paradigm. Gibson argued that we do not perceive the world as raw, meaningless geometric data that requires computational translation. Rather, we perceive the world *directly* in terms of what we can do with it.
