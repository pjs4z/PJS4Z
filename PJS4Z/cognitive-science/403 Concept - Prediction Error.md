# 403 Concept - Prediction Error

> **Definition:** In cognitive science and theoretical biology, a prediction error is the mathematical discrepancy between what a cognitive system expects to perceive and the actual sensory input it receives.

## Frame Semantic Context
In our [[300 Frame Semantics Meta-Model of Cognition]], **Prediction Error** acts as a core Frame Element within the **[[304 Frame - Predictive Inference]]**.
- It represents the mathematical delta (the **`[ERROR_SIGNAL]`**) between the **`[GENERATIVE_MODEL]`** and the **`[SENSORY_EVIDENCE]`**.

## Mechanisms of Resolution
According to Predictive Processing, the fundamental biological drive of the brain is to minimize this error signal. It utilizes a **`[RESOLUTION_POLICY]`** to achieve this in two ways:
1. **Perceptual Inference:** Update the internal model to better match the sensory evidence (changing your mind).
2. **Active Inference:** Act upon the environment with your body to change the sensory evidence to match your expected model (changing the world to fit your expectations).
