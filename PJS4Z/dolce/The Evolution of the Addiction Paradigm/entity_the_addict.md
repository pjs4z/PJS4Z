# The Addict

**Description:** The socially and medically constructed identity of the individual suffering from substance use disorder, bearing various stigmas and clinical labels across eras.

## Conceptual Coordinates
* **The Moral Degenerate (Temperance Model):** Volitional Agency (0.9), Etiological Substrate (0.85), Affective Valence (0.4), Social Integration (0.1). *Viewed through Lyman Beecher's lens, the addict is a rational actor who actively chooses to commit violence against their own nature.*
* **The Late-Stage Addict (BDMA):** Volitional Agency (0.05), Etiological Substrate (0.05), Affective Valence (0.05), Social Integration (0.2). *Locked in a permanent state of severe dysphoria and stress.*
* **The Self-Medicating Patient:** Volitional Agency (0.3), Etiological Substrate (0.9), Affective Valence (0.1), Social Integration (0.15). *Unconsciously selects a drug to cope with profound psychological suffering.*
