# Bill Wilson and Dr. Bob

**Target Action:** Founding [[entity_alcoholics_anonymous|Alcoholics Anonymous]] and authoring the Big Book. (See: [[event_founding_of_alcoholics_anonymous|Founding of Alcoholics Anonymous]])

## BDI Triad
* **Belief:** Sharing raw struggles with another alcoholic is the indispensable key to maintaining sobriety, requiring a spiritual awakening.
* **Desire:** To maintain their own precarious sobriety and provide a practical remedy for other hopeless alcoholics.
* **Intention:** To codify their peer-support mechanics and inclusive spiritual principles into a foundational text.

## Social Moments
* Shifting from isolated sufferers to founders of a global fellowship
* Bypassing dogmatic religious theology to include agnostics
* Establishing the dual framework of powerlessness and unmanageability

**Semantic Justification for Action:** Bill Wilson and Dr. Bob discovered a functional paradox: the act of focusing their energies on helping other suffering alcoholics was the indispensable mechanism for maintaining their own precarious sobriety. Codifying this with inclusive language ('God as we understand Him') was a rational strategy to bypass dogmatic theology and maximize the text's reach to agnostics and diverse sufferers.
