# The Abstaining Addict

**Target Action:** Transitioning from a minor lapse to a full relapse. (See: [[event_the_relapse_process_ave|The Relapse Process (AVE)]])

## BDI Triad
* **Belief:** A single slip represents an absolute loss of control and a catastrophic moral failure due to black-and-white thinking.
* **Desire:** To cope with immediate high-risk situations, unmanaged urges, or accumulated daily stress.
* **Intention:** To consume the substance despite prior commitments to absolute abstinence.

## Social Moments
* Breaking the social commitment to abstinence
* Internalizing the rigid moral stigma and shame of society

**Semantic Justification for Action:** Driven by the 'Abstinence Violation Effect', the addict interprets a minor slip through a rigid, black-and-white moral lens. Experiencing intense guilt and a perceived absolute loss of control, the individual rationally concludes that their sobriety is already catastrophically destroyed, making full relapse the logical behavioral response to the overwhelming cognitive dissonance and shame.
