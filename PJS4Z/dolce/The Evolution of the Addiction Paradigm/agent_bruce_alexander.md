# Bruce Alexander

**Target Action:** Conducting the [[event_rat_park_experiment|Rat Park Seduction Experiment]].

## BDI Triad
* **Belief:** Austere, stressful, and isolated environments—not the inherent chemical properties of drugs—are the primary catalysts for compulsive behavior.
* **Desire:** To expose the fatal methodological flaws in standard laboratory addiction studies and challenge pharmacological determinism.
* **Intention:** To construct an enriched, highly social housing colony to test the impact of environment on drug self-administration.

## Social Moments
* Challenging the dominant institutional Brain Disease Model
* Redefining addiction as an adaptive response to modern capitalist dislocation

**Semantic Justification for Action:** Alexander recognized a fatal methodological flaw in standard animal models: isolation cages artificially induced distress. To prove that addiction is an adaptive response to environmental dislocation rather than an inevitable chemical hijacking, it was scientifically necessary to provide an enriched social environment to observe baseline, un-coerced biological behavior.
