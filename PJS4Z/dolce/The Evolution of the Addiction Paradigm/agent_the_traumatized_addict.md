# The Traumatized Addict

**Target Action:** Engaging in compulsive substance use (Self-Medication). (See: [[event_self_medication_act|Self Medication Act]])

## BDI Triad
* **Belief:** The selected substance is the only effective mechanism to survive or mute overwhelming, disorganizing emotional pain.
* **Desire:** To assuage profound psychological suffering, internal disharmony, and the agony of unhealed trauma.
* **Intention:** To unconsciously select and consume a specific psychopharmacological agent to regulate their affective state.

## Social Moments
* Experiencing profound social and cultural dislocation
* Suffering from impaired relationship regulation and isolation
* Attempting to artificially simulate a sense of connection and purpose

**Semantic Justification for Action:** The addict is not irrationally pursuing hedonic pleasure, but rationally deploying a highly specific, functional coping mechanism. The substance is selected because its specific psychopharmacological action (e.g., heroin for rage, alcohol for isolation) effectively mutes overwhelming emotional pain, unhealed trauma, and severe deficits in self-regulation that the individual otherwise lacks the capacity to survive.
