# The Washingtonians

**Target Action:** Pioneering mutual aid and public narrative sharing. (See: [[event_washingtonian_narrative_sharing|Washingtonian Narrative Sharing]])

## BDI Triad
* **Belief:** The drunkard can be restored to a useful place in society through peer support and shared lived experience.
* **Desire:** To achieve sobriety and rescue fellow sufferers from the horrors of alcoholism.
* **Intention:** To form an egalitarian fellowship and publicly share personal narratives of addiction and recovery.

## Social Moments
* Egalitarian fellowship formation
* Facing organized criticism of 'humanism heresy' from religious institutions

**Semantic Justification for Action:** The Washingtonians rationally deduced that traditional, top-down religious moralizing was ineffective. By publicly sharing their raw, lived experiences of descent and recovery, they believed they could bypass moral superiority, build genuine identification, and prove that peer support could restore a 'hopeless' drunkard to society.
