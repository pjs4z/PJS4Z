# Mutual Aid and Narrative Recovery

**Authorial Perspective:** A pragmatic, lived-experience perspective that bypasses dogmatic theology to alleviate moral shame while demanding rigorous personal inventory.

## Frame Elements
* **Agent:** Recovering alcoholics (e.g., [[agent_the_washingtonians|The Washingtonians]], [[agent_bill_wilson_and_dr_bob|Bill Wilson, Dr. Bob]])
* **Target:** Other suffering alcoholics
* **Trigger:** The catastrophic failure of institutional treatments, the vacuum left by Prohibition, and the fatal 'mental obsession' of the disease.
* **Prior State:** The alcoholic is isolated, viewed as medically untreatable, and their life has become completely unmanageable.
* **Outcome:** Sustained sobriety and restoration to society achieved through peer support, raw narrative sharing, and a spiritual awakening.

**Missing Variables:** Professional psychiatric or neurobiological medical intervention.
