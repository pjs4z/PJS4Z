# Parasitic Transfer of Executive Function
**Authorial Perspective:** Addiction is framed not merely as an individual failing, but as an involuntary outsourcing of human agency where the addict's deficit mandates a compensatory surplus in their collateral victims.

## Frame Elements
* **Agent:** [[entity_the_addict_persona|The Addict Persona]]
* **Target:** [[entity_the_affected_orbiters|The Affected Orbiters]] (Family, Workplace, Relationships)
* **Trigger:** The addict's involuntary drop into an executive function deficit (-0.40) due to biological brain hijack and substance abuse.
* **Prior State:** A baseline state of systemic stability and balanced executive function within the social ecosystem.
* **Outcome:** The orbiters are forced into an unsustainable state of compensatory hyper-competence (+0.53) to prevent the family or workplace from systemic collapse.

## Missing Variables
* The exact social, emotional, or legal binding mechanism that forces the orbiter to stay and compensate rather than simply abandoning the collapsing system.
