# 100 Top-Level Disciplines of Cognitive Science

The semantic space of Cognitive Science is historically mapped using the **Sloan Hexagon** (created in a 1978 report to the Sloan Foundation by George A. Miller). These six top-level disciplines provide the methodological and epistemic boundaries of the field. 

1. **[[Cognitive Psychology]]**
	- *Focus:* Empirical study of human behavior and mental processes (memory, attention, problem-solving).
	- *Methodology:* Behavioral experiments, chronometry, psychophysics.
2. **[[Cognitive Neuroscience]]**
	- *Focus:* Mapping mental processes to biological neural structures and wetware.
	- *Methodology:* Neuroimaging (fMRI, EEG, PET), single-cell recording, lesion studies.
3. **[[Artificial Intelligence (Computer Science)]]**
	- *Focus:* Developing computational algorithms that replicate, simulate, or exceed human cognitive tasks.
	- *Methodology:* Machine learning, neural networks, algorithmic complexity analysis.
4. **[[Philosophy of Mind]]**
	- *Focus:* The fundamental nature of mind, consciousness, intentionality, and the mind-body problem.
	- *Methodology:* Thought experiments, conceptual engineering, logical analysis.
5. **[[Cognitive Linguistics]]**
	- *Focus:* How language interacts with human cognition, semantic structures, and how thought is externalized.
	- *Methodology:* Corpus linguistics, syntactic parsing, semantic mapping.
6. **[[Cognitive Anthropology]]**
	- *Focus:* How cultural knowledge is organized, stored, transmitted, and how it shapes cognition.
	- *Methodology:* Ethnography, cross-cultural comparative studies, evolutionary psychology.

### Semantic Intersections
The true innovation of cognitive science exists on the edges connecting these nodes. 
- *Neuroscience + Psychology* = Cognitive Neuroscience
- *Linguistics + AI* = Natural Language Processing (NLP)
- *Philosophy + AI* = Machine Ethics / AI Alignment
