# Autotelic Personality

**Definition:** A resilient psychological structure, fostered by early emotional security, that enables an individual to easily let go of the self and consistently find flow in their environment.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Autotelic Personality]] functions as a compounding psychological asset. It is the primary output of [[The Flow and Complexity Growth Loop]]. As an individual repeatedly experiences the [[Flow Experience]] and develops [[Instrumental Skills]] and [[Expressive Skills]], their capacity to voluntarily direct [[Psychic Energy]] expands. This personality type provides a powerful buffer against [[Ontological Anxiety]] and prevents the descent into [[Psychic Entropy]] when external social structures are removed. 

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Flow Experience]], early emotional security via [[Integration]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Psychic Energy]], [[Instrumental Skills]], [[Expressive Skills]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This entity acts as a foundational **Capability** driver. An individual with an [[Autotelic Personality]] requires far less external **Opportunity** scaffolding to initiate complex behaviors during unstructured free time. To build this capability in others (especially children or partners), behavioral interventions must focus on providing unconditional emotional acceptance (a form of [[Integration]]) which lowers the defensive cognitive load, freeing up [[Psychic Energy]] to be invested in exploration and [[Differentiation]].
