# Consciousness Ordering Homeostasis

**Definition:** A balancing feedback loop where the natural emergence of psychic entropy (mental chaos) creates emotional discomfort, prompting the individual to deploy psychic energy into internal structuring activities to restore cognitive order.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This system acts as the mind's self-regulation mechanism. When the environment fails to provide structure, the mind must rely on its own contents to prevent descent into painful randomness.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Psychic Entropy]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Deployment of [[Psychic Energy]], [[Daydreaming]], [[Memory]] recall, and eventually [[Mental Flow]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop relies heavily on the psychological **Capability** of the individual to self-regulate. If an individual lacks tools like structured [[Daydreaming]] or a rich [[Memory]], this balancing loop fails, and they will turn to low-effort external **Opportunities** (like TV) to artificially restore order. Teaching structured mental simulation and implementing personalized memorization protocols (e.g., memorizing family lineages or sports statistics) equips the individual with a "portable" toolkit to successfully execute this balancing loop anywhere.
