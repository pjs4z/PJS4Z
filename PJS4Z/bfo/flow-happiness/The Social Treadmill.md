# The Social Treadmill

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop where human biological vulnerabilities are exploited by societal norms, trapping individuals in an endless, escalating pursuit of external rewards that yields chronic dissatisfaction.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This vicious cycle operates at the intersection of biology, society, and psychology:
1. **[[Genetic Programming]]** provides biological vulnerabilities (e.g., drives for comfort, reproduction).
2. **[[Socialization]]** exploits these vulnerabilities to dictate external goals and rewards.
3. The pursuit of these societal rewards triggers an **[[Escalation of Expectations]]**, where desires perpetually outpace achievements.
4. This chronic dissatisfaction heavily increases **[[Ontological Anxiety]]**.
5. Seeking relief from this dread, individuals double down on pursuing culturally prescribed markers of success, thereby strengthening their reliance on **[[Socialization]]** and accelerating the treadmill.

This loop can only be disrupted by the balancing loop of **[[Autonomy Homeostasis]]**, which uses [[Optimal Experience]] to inhibit the power of [[Socialization]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[The Social Treadmill]] represents a severe **Opportunity Constraint**. The environment is so saturated with societal controls and external reward systems (the [[Cultural Shield]]) that it actively distracts an individual's [[Psychic Energy]] away from intrinsic pursuits. Breaking this loop requires a systemic redesign of incentive structures, shifting motivation away from wealth and status toward autotelic, moment-to-moment fulfillment.
