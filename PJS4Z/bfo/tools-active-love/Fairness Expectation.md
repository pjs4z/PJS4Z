# Fairness Expectation

**Definition:** A deeply ingrained, childish cognitive schema dictating that the world and other people must act equitably. The external violation of this static assumption acts as the primary psychological catalyst for entering [[The Maze]].

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Fairness Expectation]] is the foundational vulnerability in an individual's psychological system. When reality inevitably violates this schema, the resulting cognitive dissonance forcefully propels the individual into a state of obsessive rumination. It is the primary engine behind the **The Maze Entrapment** feedback loop.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** Natural human psychological conditioning. It is fundamentally decreased and dissolved by the presence of [[Outflow]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Catalyzes [[The Maze]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
- **Capability Barrier:** The rigidity of the [[Fairness Expectation]] limits an individual's *Psychological Capability* to process perceived slights objectively. Overriding this schema requires conscious effort and the deployment of targeted cognitive tools.
- **Motivation:** This expectation forces the individual to demand that the "scales of justice" be balanced before they can move on, completely stalling forward momentum in life until they realize that holding onto this demand is self-destructive.
