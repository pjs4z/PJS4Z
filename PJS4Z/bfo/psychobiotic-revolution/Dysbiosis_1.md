# Dysbiosis

**Definition:** A pathological process or systemic disruption characterized by an imbalance in the normal composition of the gut microbiota, leading to inflammation and psychological distress.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Dysbiosis]] represents the collapse of a healthy [[Hologenome]]. It actively increases the permeability of the intestinal lining, resulting in a [[Leaky Gut]]. This cascade triggers the [[Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis]] and initiates the [[Vicious Cycle of Gut-Brain Inflammation]]. Extreme dysbiosis can only be arrested by targeted interventions like [[Phage Therapy]] or a [[Fecal Microbial Transplant]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Sickness Behavior]] (via stress feedback), [[Hygiene Hypothesis]] (via environmental deprivation)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Leaky Gut]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Dysbiosis]] fundamentally degrades an individual's psychological and physical Capability. Treating or avoiding it requires Opportunity restructuring, such as modifying medical environments to default to targeted [[Phage Therapy]] instead of commensal-destroying broad-spectrum antibiotics. Furthermore, to prevent stress-induced dysbiosis, interventions must target Motivation through non-dietary stress reduction, curbing the drive to consume dysbiosis-promoting processed foods.
