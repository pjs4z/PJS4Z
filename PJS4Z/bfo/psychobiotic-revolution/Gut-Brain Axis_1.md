# Gut-Brain Axis

**Definition:** The bidirectional communication network between the digestive tract and the central nervous system, mediated through neural, immune, and endocrine pathways.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Gut-Brain Axis]] is the primary anatomical and chemical conduit that translates biological states into psychological experiences. It allows signals from a healthy [[Microbiota]] to promote mental well-being, while also permitting inflammatory markers from a [[Leaky Gut]] to cross domains and catalyze [[Sickness Behavior]]. It acts as the bridge in the [[Vicious Cycle of Gut-Brain Inflammation]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis]], [[Leaky Gut]], [[Short-Chain Fatty Acids]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Sickness Behavior]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Understanding the [[Gut-Brain Axis]] is a powerful tool for modifying human Motivation. By educating individuals on this axis, they can cognitively reframe their dietary cravings—learning to separate their own reflective goals from the automatic, pathogenic drives of a dysbiotic [[Microbiota]]. This psychological distancing increases the reflective motivation needed to adhere to a [[Psychobiotic]] regimen.
