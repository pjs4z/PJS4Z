# Sickness Behavior

**Definition:** An evolutionarily wired psychological and behavioral state resembling depression, prompting an infected individual to conserve energy and isolate from others to prevent spreading contagion.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Sickness Behavior]] is the psychological manifestation of severe gut and immune distress. It is heavily increased by [[Autointoxication]] stemming from [[Intestinal Permeability]]. In an attempt to manage this, the [[Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis]] secretes cortisol to suppress the immune system's overreaction, which temporarily attempts to restore [[Homeostasis]] and decrease the severity of the [[Sickness Behavior]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Autointoxication]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** Reduced physical activity, social isolation (acts as a behavioral sink)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Sickness Behavior]] severely limits an individual's physical and psychological Capability to enact positive lifestyle changes. Because it induces profound lethargy and depression, high-exertion interventions are likely to fail. A highly leveraged behavioral intervention is intermittent fasting (time-restricted eating), which requires zero added physical exertion—thus bypassing the capability barrier of [[Sickness Behavior]]—while autonomously promoting gut healing and restoring physiological [[Homeostasis]].
