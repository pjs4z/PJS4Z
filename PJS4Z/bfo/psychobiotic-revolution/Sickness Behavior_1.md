# Sickness Behavior

**Definition:** An evolutionarily hardwired behavioral and cognitive response to infection characterized by lethargy, anxiety, and social withdrawal to conserve energy; it can manifest as chronic depression if prolonged.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Sickness Behavior]] is the psychological output of biological distress. It is increased cross-domain by the activation of the [[Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis]] via the [[Gut-Brain Axis]]. Conversely, it is decreased by the soothing presence of [[Short-Chain Fatty Acids]]. If left unchecked, the stress generated by sickness behavior creates a feedback loop that increases further [[Dysbiosis]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis]], [[Gut-Brain Axis]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Dysbiosis]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Sickness Behavior]] severely limits physical Capability and reflective Motivation. To combat this, behavioral interventions must require extremely low friction. Supplying a simple [[Psychobiotic]] supplement bypasses the lethargy associated with this state. Additionally, down-regulating the [[Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis]] via targeted exercise can reduce the neuroendocrine drive that forces the patient toward anxiety-soothing but microbiota-harming foods.
