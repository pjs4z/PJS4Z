# Psychobiotic

**Definition:** A live organism, typically a beneficial bacterium like Bifidobacterium or Lactobacillus, that produces a health benefit in patients suffering from psychiatric illness or mood disorders when ingested in adequate amounts.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The introduction of a [[Psychobiotic]] to the [[Microbiota]] actively inhibits the pathogenic process of [[Dysbiosis]]. As these beneficial bacteria metabolize complex fibers, they increase the production of [[Short-Chain Fatty Acids]] (like butyrate). This drives the [[Psychobiotic Healing Cycle]], which subsequently decreases [[Intestinal Permeability]] and helps to restore [[Homeostasis]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Prebiotics]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Short-Chain Fatty Acids]], [[Homeostasis]] (and negatively drives/inhibits [[Dysbiosis]])

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
In the context of behavioral change, adopting a [[Psychobiotic]] diet acts as a profound Capability and Motivation enhancer. Initially using psychobiotic supplements artificially boosts [[Short-Chain Fatty Acids]]. This targeted physiological shift dampens systemic [[Autointoxication]] and reduces the lethargy of [[Sickness Behavior]]. By restoring a baseline state of [[Homeostasis]], the individual gains the cognitive bandwidth and physical energy needed to plan and sustain long-term dietary changes away from a [[Western Diet]].
