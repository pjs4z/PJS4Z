# Psychobiotic

**Definition:** A live organism, typically a commensal bacterium, that when ingested in adequate amounts produces a health or mood benefit in patients suffering from psychiatric illnesses.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The introduction of a [[Psychobiotic]] directly increases the health and diversity of the [[Microbiota]], serving as a foundational element in the [[Psychobiotic Homeostasis Loop]]. By flourishing in the gut, these microbes inhibit [[Dysbiosis]] and catalyze [[Fermentation]] of dietary fibers. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Fermentation]] (as a culturing process), Prebiotic diets
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Microbiota]], [[Short-Chain Fatty Acids]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
A [[Psychobiotic]] serves as a critical intervention tool to bypass deficits in Capability and Motivation caused by mental distress. Because [[Sickness Behavior]] limits a patient's cognitive and physical energy, providing structured, low-effort psychobiotic meal plans or supplements bypasses the behavioral friction of complex dietary overhauls, allowing automatic biological healing to take place.
