# Pathogenic Leaky Gut Spiral

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop where a poor diet drives gut imbalance and structural breakdown, causing systemic toxicity and stress, which in turn drives further maladaptive eating habits and gut imbalance.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
In this reinforcing loop, the consumption of a [[Western Diet]] increases [[Dysbiosis]], which structurally degrades the gut lining, increasing [[Intestinal Permeability]]. This barrier failure allows pathogens into the blood, escalating [[Autointoxication]]. The [[Gut-Brain Axis]] detects this systemic threat and activates the [[Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis]]. The resulting chronic stress, inflammation, and altered mood drive further [[Dysbiosis]] and psychological cravings for the very [[Western Diet]] that initiated the cycle.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Western Diet]], [[Dysbiosis]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Intestinal Permeability]], [[Autointoxication]], [[Sickness Behavior]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
To engineer an exit from this destructive loop, individuals must target the physical Opportunity drivers. Completely removing the [[Western Diet]] from the immediate environment disrupts the loop at its source. Over time, combining this with [[Prebiotics]] and a [[Psychobiotic]] protocol transitions the system from this pathogenic spiral into the balancing and restorative [[Psychobiotic Healing Cycle]].
