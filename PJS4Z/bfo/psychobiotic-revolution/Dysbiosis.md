# Dysbiosis

**Definition:** The process of pathogenic imbalance or disruption within the microbial community of the gut, which compromises immune defense and is significantly linked to physical disease, depression, and anxiety.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Dysbiosis]] represents a critical structural breakdown within the [[Microbiota]]. It directly increases [[Intestinal Permeability]] (leaky gut), allowing toxins to seep into the bloodstream. This event cascades into systemic [[Autointoxication]] and triggers the [[Gut-Brain Axis]] to activate the [[Hypothalamic-Pituitary-Adrenal Axis]]. It is the central engine of the [[Pathogenic Leaky Gut Spiral]]. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Western Diet]], [[Hygiene Hypothesis]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Intestinal Permeability]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
[[Dysbiosis]] acts as a severe barrier to healthy behavior by hijacking automatic Motivation. Dysbiotic microbes interact with the [[Enteric Nervous System]] to induce maladaptive cravings for high-sugar, high-fat components of the [[Western Diet]]. Breaking this cycle requires modifying physical Opportunity—namely, completely removing processed foods from the environment to starve the pathogenic bacteria and disrupt the cravings they chemically induce.
