# Dopamine Dysregulation

**Definition:** A severe neurobiological pathology resulting from the overstimulation of the mesolimbic pathway, causing a fundamental alteration in the baseline valuation of natural rewards and the specific computational mechanisms for predicting the future.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Dopamine Dysregulation]] acts as the core biological engine driving the pathology of time perception. Chronic exposure to unpredicted, massive pharmacological dopamine surges overstimulates the [[Frontostriatal Circuitry]]. This forces a homeostatic biological response that blunts sensitivity to normal stimuli, effectively distorting how the brain computes the future. It is a critical node in the [[Addiction-Temporal Truncation Cycle]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Preference Reversal]] (increases)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[D2 Receptor Downregulation]] (increases), [[Reward Prediction Error (RPE)]] (inhibits)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This occurrent serves as a profoundly distorted **Motivation** driver. By inhibiting normative [[Reward Prediction Error (RPE)]] signals, it systematically destroys an individual's motivation to pursue abstract, delayed rewards (like health or career). Treating this biological deficit strictly through traditional "talk therapy" often fails because the patient's biological motivation system is functionally blind to distant goals. Interventions must use environmental **Opportunity** structures like [[Contingency Management (CM)]] to provide immediate, highly salient rewards that can compete with hyper-sensitized drug cues.
