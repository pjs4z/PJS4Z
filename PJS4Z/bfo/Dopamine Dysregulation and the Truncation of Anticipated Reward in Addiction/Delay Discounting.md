# Delay Discounting

**Definition:** A behavioral economic process in which the subjective value of a specific reward undergoes hyperbolic decay as a direct function of the temporal delay required to receive it.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Delay Discounting]] translates unseen neurobiological deficits into observable, quantifiable behavioral economic choices. Driven heavily by a shrunken [[Temporal Horizon]] and exacerbated by [[Reinforcer Pathology]] and [[Delay Aversion]], steep discounting rates guarantee that delayed consequences (both positive rewards and negative punishments) are mathematically reduced to near-zero subjective value in the moment of decision-making. 
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Temporal Horizon]] (decreases), [[D2 Receptor Downregulation]] (increases), [[Reinforcer Pathology]] (increases), [[Delay Aversion]] (increases), [[Neural Coupling]] (decreases)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Preference Reversal]] (increases)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Steep [[Delay Discounting]] is the ultimate **Motivation** barrier to delaying gratification. It structurally guarantees that an individual will choose immediate, smaller rewards (drugs) over larger, later rewards (sobriety). Behavioral leverage points include artificially attenuating this decay via **Capability**-enhancing cognitive exercises like [[Episodic Future Thinking (EFT)]], or altering the choice architecture through semantic framing (e.g., Explicit-Zero Framing or Date Versus Delay Framing) to shift temporal attention forward without requiring high executive effort.
