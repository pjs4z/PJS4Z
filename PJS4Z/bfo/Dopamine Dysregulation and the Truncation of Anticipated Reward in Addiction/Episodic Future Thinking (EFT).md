# Episodic Future Thinking (EFT)

**Definition:** A targeted cognitive intervention that requires individuals to vividly imagine and generate distinct cognitive tags for highly positive future events, successfully artificially extending their temporal horizon and attenuating delay discounting.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Episodic Future Thinking (EFT)]] is a top-down, executive-driven process that rehabilitates anticipatory networks. By forcing the brain to simulate the future, it actively restores connectivity within the brain, overriding the blunted signals typically caused by chronic addiction.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** N/A (Acts as an exogenous clinical intervention)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Neural Coupling]] (catalyzes)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
EFT functions as a premier **Capability** enhancer in behavioral design. By anchoring the future to vivid, emotionally resonant, self-generated episodic memories-in-reverse, it restores the psychological capability to feel and value delayed rewards. This intervention directly counteracts steep [[Delay Discounting]] and bypasses the constraints of a restricted [[Temporal Horizon]], allowing the individual's executive centers to functionally compete with immediate, habitual drug cravings.
