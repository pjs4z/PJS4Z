# Addiction-Temporal Truncation Cycle

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop where the preference for immediate drug consumption drives neurobiological overstimulation, which shrinks the psychological temporal horizon, subsequently accelerating delay discounting and mathematically guaranteeing future impulsivity.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This reinforcing loop maps the catastrophic synergy between neurobiology and behavioral economics. The cycle triggers when an immediate drug choice ([[Preference Reversal]]) induces massive [[Dopamine Dysregulation]]. To maintain homeostasis, the brain initiates [[D2 Receptor Downregulation]]. This biological deficit structurally decreases the cognitive [[Temporal Horizon]] (restricting it to mere days). A restricted temporal horizon mathematically accelerates [[Delay Discounting]], which in turn drives the next instance of [[Preference Reversal]], trapping the organism in an accelerating cycle of myopic decision-making.
- **System Components:** [[Preference Reversal]] ➔ [[Dopamine Dysregulation]] ➔ [[D2 Receptor Downregulation]] ➔ [[Temporal Horizon]] ➔ [[Delay Discounting]] ➔ [[Preference Reversal]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Understanding this systemic trap is crucial for designing effective interventions. Spontaneous recovery is exceedingly rare because the loop degrades both **Motivation** (via blunted [[Reward Prediction Error (RPE)]]) and **Capability** (via a shrunken [[Temporal Horizon]]). To break the cycle, engineers must inject external **Opportunity** leverage—such as [[Contingency Management (CM)]]—to provide an immediate, competitive stimulus that short-circuits [[Preference Reversal]] before the neurobiological cascade can fire again.
