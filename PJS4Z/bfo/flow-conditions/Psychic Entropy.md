# Psychic Entropy

**Definition:** A state of internal disorder within consciousness, typically experienced as boredom or anxiety, arising when external challenges and internal skills are mismatched or when attentional control fails.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Psychic Entropy]] is the default state of an undirected mind and the primary antagonist to [[Flow]]. It is exacerbated systemically by macro-level societal dysfunctions such as [[Anomie]] (lack of rules causing anxiety) and [[Alienation]] (systemic constraints causing boredom), as well as biological deficits like [[Stimulus Overinclusion]]. However, the discomfort of [[Psychic Entropy]] serves as a critical negative feedback signal within the [[Flow Complexity and Self-Growth Loop]], pushing an individual to either increase their skills or seek higher challenges to restore cognitive order.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Anomie]], [[Alienation]], [[Stimulus Overinclusion]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Flow Complexity and Self-Growth Loop]] (as a negative driver), [[Flow]] (inhibits)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
From a motivational standpoint, [[Psychic Entropy]] acts as a negative **Motivation** driver—individuals are driven to escape the negative affect of boredom and anxiety. When designing systems, identifying where users experience high psychic entropy highlights the exact points where **Opportunity** (via clear rules or better [[Flow Activities]]) or **Capability** (via skill-building) must be injected to rescue user engagement and transition them back into an optimal state.
