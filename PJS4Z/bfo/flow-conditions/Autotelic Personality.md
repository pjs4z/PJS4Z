# Autotelic Personality

**Definition:** A characterological disposition marked by nonself-conscious individualism and intrinsic motivation, enabling a person to effortlessly restructure consciousness and find enjoyment even in highly adverse environments.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
The [[Autotelic Personality]] is the structural foundation for internally generated [[Flow]]. Individuals with this trait possess high [[Attentional Flexibility]], allowing them to screen out irrelevant noise and avoid [[Stimulus Overinclusion]]. Systemically, this personality profile is often catalyzed early in life by an [[Autotelic Family Context]], which conserves psychic energy by minimizing domestic conflict. Over a lifetime, it is continually reinforced by the [[Flow Complexity and Self-Growth Loop]], as the individual habituates to seeking out complex challenges.

- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Autotelic Family Context]], [[Flow Complexity and Self-Growth Loop]]
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Flow]], [[Attentional Flexibility]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This trait acts as a profound **Motivation** and **Capability** driver. Because autotelic individuals are intrinsically motivated, they are highly resilient against societal [[Anomie]] and systemic [[Alienation]]. To cultivate this in a population, behavioral interventions should focus on cognitive-behavioral training that teaches individuals to identify intrinsic rewards, set self-directed micro-goals, and reframe mundane or adverse situations as controllable challenges.
