# Active Love

**Definition:** A directed cognitive-emotional practice used to break cycles of resentment by intentionally summoning and channeling a profound, universal sense of love toward a specific stressor or individual.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Active Love]] functions as an active behavioral intervention designed to disrupt negative psychological homeostasis. It directly targets and decreases the obsessive loops characteristic of [[The Maze]]. When executed effectively, it serves as a catalyst, connecting the individual to [[Higher Forces]], which in turn drives the broader process of [[Inner Evolution]]. However, the practice is frequently inhibited by a rigid [[Mechanistic Worldview]]. It is a primary mechanism within the [[Maze Disruption and Homeostasis]] balancing loop.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[Spiritual System]], [[The Maze]] (when reframed as a cue)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Higher Forces]], [[The Maze]] (decreases/disrupts), [[Inner Evolution]]

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
Practicing [[Active Love]] is the core target behavior for breaking resentment cycles.
- **Capability:** Severely inhibited by a [[Mechanistic Worldview]] and the cognitive load of [[The Maze]]. To bypass this, the behavior must be decoupled from belief; the user should be instructed to apply the tool mechanically ("shut up and use the tool") to lower the barrier to entry.
- **Opportunity:** Environmental stressors and the onset of [[The Maze]] act as prime temporal opportunities (triggers) to deploy the tool. The use of simple visual frameworks (like the 3x5 index card of the [[Spiritual System]]) reduces friction and creates a tangible opportunity to practice when bandwidth is low.
- **Motivation:** Sustained practice is driven by the immediate affective reward of experiencing [[Higher Forces]], which retroactively dismantles skepticism and fuels ongoing [[Inner Evolution]].
