# Psychic Entropy

**Definition:** The internal disorder experienced within consciousness when random, unorganized information interferes with an individual's goals.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
[[Psychic Entropy]] represents the default state of consciousness when the [[Mind-Body System]] is left untrained or passive. It accumulates when [[Psychic Energy]] is depleted, often as a result of [[The Hedonic Trap]] (chasing effortless [[Pleasure]]). It acts as a powerful inhibitor of [[Flow]]. To counteract it, individuals engage in the **Entropy Regulation Cycle**, utilizing structured practices like [[Hatha Yoga]] to focus attention, catalyze [[Flow]], and restore harmony to the mind.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** [[The Hedonic Trap]], Unstructured [[Mind-Body System]], [[Pleasure]] (indirectly)
- **Downstream (Drives):** [[Flow]] (inhibits), [[Enjoyment]] (decreases)

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
The accumulation of [[Psychic Entropy]] serves as a negative **Motivation** driver (the drive to avoid internal chaos, boredom, and anxiety).
- **Leverage Point:** Use the data gathered via tools like the [[Experience Sampling Method]] to visually demonstrate to the user the direct correlation between passive [[Pleasure]] seeking and the accumulation of [[Psychic Entropy]]. By rendering the invisible [[The Hedonic Trap]] visible, behavioral engineers can foster a strong reflective motivation to invest [[Psychic Energy]] into more complex, growth-oriented tasks.
