# Autotelic Growth Cycle

**Definition:** A reinforcing system loop where an individual marshals and directs attention toward environmental challenges, catalyzing Flow. As the individual sustains Flow, they merge with their environment and overcome challenges, which increases the complexity and resilience of the self, driving further capacity for Flow.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This is the primary growth engine of human consciousness, functioning as a Reinforcing Loop. It relies on the [[Autotelic Self]] utilizing [[Psychic Energy]] to engage with the world.
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Clear goals, matched challenges and skills, and continuous feedback. Often initiated by [[Transformational Coping]] when under duress.
- **Downstream (Drives):** Increases the complexity of the [[Autotelic Self]] and continuously expands the individual's capacity to experience [[Flow]]. Operates in direct opposition to the [[Regressive Entropy Spiral]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
To maintain this loop, the environment must constantly supply the correct **Opportunity** (well-matched challenges) to trigger **Automatic Motivation** (intrinsic enjoyment).
- **Leverage Point:** Engineering *Feedback-Rich Action Systems* allows individuals to effortlessly match their current skills to new challenges. If the loop stalls (e.g., a challenge becomes too hard, threatening [[Psychic Entropy]]), *Micro-Goal Scaffolding* can step the challenge down to manageable levels to keep the cycle reinforcing.
