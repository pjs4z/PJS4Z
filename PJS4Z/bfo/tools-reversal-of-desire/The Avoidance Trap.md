# The Avoidance Trap

**Definition:** A reinforcing feedback loop wherein the presence of pain triggers a conditioned reliance on immediate gratification, which rapidly builds the comfort zone and systematically erodes the individual's baseline tolerance for adversity.

## Systems Dynamics & Relationships
This loop represents the pathology of modern stagnation. As the [[Comfort Zone]] expands, the individual's psychological resilience drops, which effectively amplifies the perceived intensity of future [[Pain]].
- **Upstream (Driven by):** Initiated by the interaction between baseline [[Pain]] and ubiquitous access to [[Immediate Gratification]].
- **Downstream (Drives):** Leads to a systemic paralysis, neutralizing the [[Force of Forward Motion]] and fostering a persistent [[Victim Mentality]].

## Behavioral Engineering Context (COM-B)
This loop degrades both Capability (loss of psychological fortitude) and Motivation (overwritten by dopaminergic soothing). Breaking this loop requires a dual approach: applying the [[Reversal of Desire]] to invert the response to [[Pain]], and utilizing Friction Architecture to cut off the Opportunity pathways to [[Immediate Gratification]].
