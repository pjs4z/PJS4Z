# D&S: The Disease Model vs. Moral Agency in Narrative Media

**Generated Situation:** Fictional addicts (e.g., Whip Whitaker, Don Birnam) experience total executive collapse and neurobiological hijacking, yet are ultimately forced to confess and face consequences.

**Perspectival Bias:** Clinical frameworks view the addict's actions deterministically as symptoms of a hijacked brain, whereas Western cultural narratives demand moral agency, viewing the same actions as ethical transgressions requiring accountability and redemption.
