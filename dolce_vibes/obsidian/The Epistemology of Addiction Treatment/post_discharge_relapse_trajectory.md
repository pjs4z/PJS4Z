# Post-Discharge Relapse Trajectory
## Mereology (Temporal Phases)
* **Pre-State:** A patient completes an acute 28-day inpatient rehabilitation program and is discharged into the community, often without [[medication_assisted_treatment_mat|Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT)]].
* **Execution:** The patient navigates the first 3 to 6 months post-discharge, facing profound alterations in brain circuitry, intense cravings, and potential social insecurities without the protective environment of inpatient care.
* **Post-State:** The patient experiences a degradation of abstinence, culminating in a clinical [[patient_relapse|relapse]], confirming survival analysis data that the highest frequency of failure occurs immediately following acute treatment cessation.
