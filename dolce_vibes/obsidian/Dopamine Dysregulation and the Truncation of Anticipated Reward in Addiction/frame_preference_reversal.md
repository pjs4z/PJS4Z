# Preference Reversal Relapse Frame

**Authorial Perspective:** Viewed as a predictable mathematical outcome of a steep discounting curve intercepting a proximal reward, rather than a failure of moral intent.

## Frame Elements
* **Agent:** [[Addicted_Individual|Addicted individual in recovery]]
* **Target:** Immediate drug of abuse (Smaller-Sooner Reward)
* **Trigger:** Closing temporal distance to the opportunity to consume the drug
* **Prior State:** Genuine commitment to sustained abstinence and high valuation of the abstract Larger-Later Reward (LLR)
* **Outcome:** Subjective value of the immediate drug skyrockets, eclipsing the LLR, precipitating sudden relapse *(See: [[Preference_Reversal_Relapse|Preference Reversal Event]])*

**Missing Variables:** Explicit conscious deliberation or moral choice at the moment of relapse.
