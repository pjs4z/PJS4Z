# Sensory Cue Reactivity and Compulsive Consumption

**Authorial Perspective:** Framed as a biological hijacking of anticipatory mechanisms by the modern food environment, mimicking pharmacological dopamine spikes.

## Frame Elements
* **Agent:** Individual with clinical obesity or binge eating disorder
* **Target:** Highly processed, ultra-palatable foods
* **Trigger:** Intense sensory input (olfactory or visual cues like bakery smells or digital ads)
* **Prior State:** Maintenance of long-term dietary intentions
* **Outcome:** Massive anticipatory dopamine release, instant narrowing of the [[Temporal_Horizon|temporal horizon]], and urgent compulsive food-seeking behavior

**Missing Variables:** Higher-order executive control or conscious dietary planning during the cue exposure.
