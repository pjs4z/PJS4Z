# Moral Accountability and Confession
**Authorial Perspective:** Dramatically necessary moralism; insists on ultimate ethical responsibility despite the deterministic pull of the neurobiological [[disease_model|disease model]].

## Frame Elements
* **Agent:** The Addict seeking redemption (e.g., [[agent_whip_whitaker|Whip Whitaker]], [[agent_jesse_pinkman|Jesse Pinkman]])
* **Target:** The truth, the victims, or the legal/social institution
* **Trigger:** A moment of profound ethical clarity or overwhelming guilt over collateral harm caused to innocents
* **Prior State:** Living in secondary denial, deception, or moral decay within an illicit economy
* **Outcome:** Reclamation of moral agency, acceptance of punishment (e.g., imprisonment), and tragic redemption

**Missing Variables:** The neurobiological cure (accountability resolves the moral arc but does not erase the biological disease)
