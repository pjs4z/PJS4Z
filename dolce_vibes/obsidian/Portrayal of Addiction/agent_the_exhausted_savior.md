# The Exhausted Savior
**Target Action:** Dedicating their life to preventing the addict's world from collapsing.

## BDI Triad
* **Belief:** Believes that without their constant, hyper-competent intervention, the entire family or relationship system will collapse.
* **Desire:** Desires to save the addict, preserve systemic stability, and prevent the ultimate loss of their loved one.
* **Intention:** Commits to a life of extreme organization and compensatory executive function to offset the addict's chaos.

## Intentional Relations (Dennett's Intentional Stance)
* **Is Caused By Intention:** Commits to a life of extreme organization and compensatory executive function to offset the addict's chaos.
* **Semantic Justification:** The agent operates under the rationale that their hyper-competence is the sole barrier preventing total systemic collapse of the family or relationship, accepting anticipatory grief and trauma as the necessary cost of preserving the addict's life.

## Social Moments
* Bearing the burden of anticipatory grief
* Granted a halo of tragic virtue as the audience's moral anchor
* Subjected to the parasitic transfer of executive function
