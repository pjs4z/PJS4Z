# Neutralization of Moral Villainy
**Authorial Perspective:** Modern media walks a tightrope where the addict is morally neutralized (-0.22) by the disease model, which structurally requires the orbiters to be elevated to a halo of tragic virtue (+0.56) to serve as moral anchors.

## Frame Elements
* **Agent:** The [[entity_clinical_trauma_model|Clinical/Trauma Narrative Framework]]
* **Target:** [[entity_the_addict_persona|The Addict]] and [[entity_the_affected_orbiters|The Affected Orbiters]]
* **Trigger:** The cultural transition from the [[entity_moral_model|Moral Model]] to the [[entity_clinical_trauma_model|Clinical/Trauma Model]] of addiction in storytelling.
* **Prior State:** The historical framing where the alcoholic was viewed as a pure villain (MOR: -1.0) driven by inherent badness.
* **Outcome:** The addict is absolved of inherent malice, and the orbiters are granted undeniable moral superiority and innocence.

## Missing Variables
* The addict's internal cognitive appraisal of their own moral absolution or guilt regarding the harm they cause.
