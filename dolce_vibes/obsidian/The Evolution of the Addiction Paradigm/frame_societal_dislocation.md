# Societal Dislocation and Adaptation

**Authorial Perspective:** A macro-sociological, anti-reductionist lens that views addiction as a structural political failure requiring societal reintegration.

## Frame Elements
* **Agent:** Modern free-market capitalism and the forces of globalization
* **Target:** The dislocated individual and traditional communities
* **Trigger:** The aggressive dismantling of extended families, native cultures, and social safety nets in the pursuit of economic efficiency.
* **Prior State:** A fundamental human need for profound psychosocial integration, belonging, meaning, and identity.
* **Outcome:** Widespread 'poverty of the spirit' where individuals desperately cling to addictive behaviors to artificially simulate a sense of connection and purpose.

**Missing Variables:** The inherent, deterministic chemical addictiveness of the drug (argued to be secondary to the environmental context).
