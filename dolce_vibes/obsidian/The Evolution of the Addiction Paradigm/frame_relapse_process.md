# The Cognitive-Behavioral Relapse Process

**Authorial Perspective:** A nuanced, behaviorist, and cognitive perspective that views addiction as a learned behavior and relapse as a correctable process rather than an absolute biological failure.

## Frame Elements
* **Agent:** [[agent_the_abstaining_addict|The abstaining individual]]
* **Target:** The substance and the individual's own cognitive behavioral patterns
* **Trigger:** High-risk situations, poor coping skills, and accumulated daily stress.
* **Prior State:** A precarious state of abstinence complicated by broader lifestyle imbalances and unmanaged urges.
* **Outcome:** A minor lapse transforms into a full, catastrophic relapse due to the cognitive interpretation of the slip (the [[vibe_ave_guilt_spiral|Abstinence Violation Effect]]).

**Missing Variables:** The deterministic neurobiological brain changes (focus is shifted entirely to cognitive framing and behavioral control).
