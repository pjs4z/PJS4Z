# Incentive-Sensitized Craving (The 'Wanting' Spike)

## Emotion Frame Elements
* **Experiencer:** [[entity_the_addict|The individual with a history of chronic drug exposure.]]
* **Trigger:** Environmental cues associated with past drug use (e.g., the sight of a syringe, the smell of a bar, or internal states of stress).
* **Appraisal:** An overwhelming, non-rational motivational drive: 'I absolutely must have this substance right now, regardless of the catastrophic consequences or my lack of actual enjoyment of it.'
* **Physiological Substrate:** Pathological hyper-sensitization of the mesolimbic dopamine system, creating potentiated spikes of incentive salience ('wanting') independent of hedonic hotspots ('liking').
* **Social Display Rule:** The medical and social expectation that physical detox cures addiction is violated, as the individual is normatively expected to be 'cured' once withdrawal ends, masking the invisible, enduring neurological vulnerability to cues.
