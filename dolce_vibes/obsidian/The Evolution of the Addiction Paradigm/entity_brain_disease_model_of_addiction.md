# Brain Disease Model of Addiction

**Description:** A dominant institutional framework asserting that addiction is a chronic, relapsing brain disorder driven by neuroadaptations in reward and stress circuitries.

## Contextual D&S Pattern
* **Description Framework:** Brain Disease Model of Addiction (Volkow & Koob)
* **Generated Situation:** The addicted individual is treated as a patient suffering from a broken brain mechanism, locked in a progressive cycle of bingeing, withdrawal, and preoccupation.
* **Perspectival Bias:** A highly neuro-centric, reductionist biological narrative that emphasizes physical brain pathology over environmental, social, or psychological contexts.
